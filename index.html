Learn German: A1 → B2

Durations Resources Daily routines Speaking Writing All links

This guide outlines how to study German from A1 to B2. Timelines can vary. The author passed the Goethe B2 exam after focused preparation.

Durations

  • A1: 2 weeks – 1 month
  • A2: 2 weeks – 1 month
  • B1: 1 – 3 months
  • B2: 1 – 3 months (≈1–1.5 months grammar, then exam practice)

Daily routines

A1 (4–6 hours/day, 2 weeks – 1 month)

  • 1 pm study block:
    • Grammar (plan videos across your timeframe)
    • Anki (30–120 cards/day; use Custom Study to add more)
    • Read news or watch German content (use double subs if helpful)
    • Practice speaking (400 topics)
  • Evening: free time; rest and recover
  • 2 am: sleep

A2 (4–6 hours/day, 2 weeks – 1 month)

  • 1 pm study block (same structure as A1)
  • Evening: free time
  • 2 am: sleep

B1 (6–8 hours/day, 1 – 3 months)

  • 1 pm study block:
    • Grammar
    • Anki
    • Read news / Watch YouTube
    • Speaking (400)
  • Evening: free time
  • 2 am: sleep

B2 part 1 (6–8 hours/day, 1 – 1.5 months)

  • 1 pm study block:
    • Grammar
    • Anki
    • Read news / Watch content
    • Speaking (400)
  • Evening: free time
  • 2 am: sleep

B2 part 2 (6–8 hours/day, 1.5 – 2+ months)

Alternate days between skills:

  • Day A: Reading + Writing (two writing tasks: forum + letter)
  • Day B: Listening + Speaking (four short presentations + two debates)

How to practice speaking

  • Learn reusable phrases (e.g., from Mit Erfolg) and stick to 2–3.
  • Presentation (≈5 mins):
    • Early weeks: paste prompts into a model, read aloud, then recreate without looking.
    • Later: template = intro → 3 examples (1 pro, 2 cons each) → choose one → summary.
  • Debate:
    • Prepare 4–5 pros and 4–5 cons per topic.
    • Memorize core phrases (e.g., “Ich stimme dir zu”, “Meiner Meinung nach …”).

How to practice writing

  • Generate B2-level examples; if too hard, ask for simpler but still B2.
  • For first weeks: rewrite each sentence from memory 2–3× (not entire paragraphs).
  • Later: write original answers using learned structures.
